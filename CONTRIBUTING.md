# Contributing

Contributions are welcome when they improve reproducibility, clarity, limitations, or educational value.

## Good Contributions

Good contributions include:

- Toy quantum circuits
- Simulator-first examples
- Reproducible notebooks
- Baseline comparisons
- Noise-model documentation
- Limitation-focused analysis
- Clear separation between simulator and hardware results
- Safer language around experimental claims

## Not Accepted

Do not submit:

- Private system internals
- Proprietary architecture details
- Secrets, tokens, credentials, or `.env` files
- Private account identifiers
- Unredacted provider job metadata that should remain private
- Claims of quantum speedup without strong reproducible evidence
- Claims of production AI relevance without clear qualification

## Contribution Standard

Every contribution should answer:

1. What is the experiment testing?
2. Is the input synthetic or public?
3. Can someone reproduce it?
4. What backend or simulator was used?
5. What are the limitations?
6. What should readers not infer from the result?

## Tone

Be careful and evidence-first. This repository is exploratory. It should not hype quantum advantage, AI capability, or production relevance beyond what the evidence supports.
